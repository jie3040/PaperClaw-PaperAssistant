\section{Conclusion}

VibSemAlign advances vibration fault diagnosis through semantic alignment of spectrograms and fault descriptions via refined vision-language models, augmented by contrastive losses and MAML. Evaluations on CWRU and Paderborn affirm zero/few-shot robustness amid data scarcity and shifts.

Contributions: VSCL bridges visuals/texts for subtle signatures; cross-attention/MAML enables minimal-sample adaptation (+17.2 pp zero-shot); interpretable semantics exceed signals.

Future: multimodal fusion; edge real-time (Jetson 22 Hz); large datasets, unsupervised prompts.

VibSemAlign heralds semantic maintenance, curbing downtime in manufacturing/energy/transport.
