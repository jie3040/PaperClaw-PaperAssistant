\begin{abstract}
Zero-shot fault diagnosis addresses the critical challenge of identifying novel fault types without labeled training data. This paper proposes CESM-Diff (CLIP-Enhanced Semantic Manifold Diffusion), a novel framework that leverages CLIP text encoders and dual-path diffusion to synthesize discriminative features for unseen fault classes. CESM-Diff introduces three key innovations: (1) a Semantic Manifold Interpolation (SMI) module that constructs continuous fault representations in CLIP embedding space, (2) a dual-path diffusion architecture that simultaneously refines feature and semantic paths with cross-path attention, and (3) an Attribute Consistency Loss that ensures generated features maintain physical plausibility. Extensive experiments on three industrial benchmarks (TEP, Hydraulic System, CWRU Bearing) demonstrate that CESM-Diff achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot fault diagnosis accuracy of 91.3\% on CWRU, 82.4\% on TEP, and 79.8\% on Hydraulic, significantly outperforming existing methods.
\end{abstract}
