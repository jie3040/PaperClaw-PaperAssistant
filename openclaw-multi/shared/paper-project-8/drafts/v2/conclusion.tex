\section{Conclusion}
This paper presents the Cross-Modality Semantic Alignment Transformer (CMSA-Trans), which represents a new approach to the zero-shot fault diagnosis of rotating machinery. By using the expansive, high-dimensional linguistic knowledge from Large Language Models (LLMs), CMSA-Trans effectively removes the traditional dependency on labor-intensive and error-prone manual attribute labeling. The integration of a Time Series Transformer (TST) encoder with a cross-attention mechanism enables smooth alignment of raw vibration signal features with semantic fault prototypes generated by LLMs. Experimental results on two datasets, Case Western Reserve University (CWRU) and Southeast University (SEU), show that CMSA-Trans not only achieves high accuracy in zero-shot scenarios but also shows reliable generalization across unseen fault types. This research shows that bridging the gap between physical sensor data and large-scale semantic descriptors is an effective strategy for intelligent industrial maintenance. Future work will focus on expanding the framework to multi-source domain adaptation to further improve cross-machine reliability and investigating the deployment of lightweight CMSA-Trans variants for edge computing environments, enabling real-time, on-device diagnostic capabilities in industrial Internet of Things (IoT) networks.
